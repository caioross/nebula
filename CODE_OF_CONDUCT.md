# Code of Conduct

## The short version

Argue about ideas, never about people. Assume competence and good faith. Write to be understood, not to win.

## Expectations

Participants in Nebula's spaces — issues, pull requests, discussions and any project channel — are expected to:

- Criticize designs, proposals and code on their merits, with reasons.
- Accept that rejection of an idea is not rejection of its author.
- Keep discussions on topic and free of self-promotion.
- Respect that maintainers' time is finite; do your reading before asking.
- Welcome newcomers. Everyone's first RFC has rough edges.

The following are not tolerated: personal attacks, harassment of any kind, discriminatory remarks, sustained disruption of discussions, and publishing others' private information.

## Enforcement

Violations are handled by the project lead. Depending on severity, responses range from a private note to a permanent ban. Reports can be sent by email to the maintainer listed on the project's GitHub profile; reports are kept confidential.

There is no appeals bureaucracy. Decisions are made by a person, explained plainly, and revisited if the facts warrant it.
